  ### Collection: For Engineers
---

In 2012, Knight Capital reused an old, un-retired feature flag for new code. When a deploy went out unevenly, that flag switched on long-dead code on some servers — and in about three-quarters of an hour it ran up roughly a $440M loss and effectively ended the company. The lesson isn't "flags are dangerous." It's "stale flags are dangerous." Retire them.
